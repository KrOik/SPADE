Key Features

An explanation of the core functionalities of the SPADE platform:

AMP Sequence Generation

Generate new potential antimicrobial peptide sequences based on advanced AI models trained on extensive biological datasets.

Activity Prediction

Evaluate the antimicrobial activity of input sequences against specific pathogens with high predictive accuracy.

Toxicity Assessment

Predict the potential toxicity of peptide sequences to host cells, helping identify safe therapeutic candidates.

Physicochemical Analysis

Calculate key parameters such as molecular weight, isoelectric point, and hydrophobicity for peptide sequences.

Database Integration

Provide access to and comparison with existing AMP databases, allowing researchers to contextualize their findings within current scientific literature and known sequences.

Platform Workflow Overview

Operating Procedures

How to use the SPADE platform effectively:

1

Registration & Login

Access the platform and create an account or log in to your existing account. New users receive a 14-day trial of premium features.

2

Function Selection

Choose your desired function from the main navigation or dashboard, such as "Sequence Generation" or "Activity Prediction."

3

Parameter Input

Depending on the selected function, input necessary parameters like target bacterial species, sequence length constraints, and activity thresholds.

Tip: For optimal results in sequence generation, specify both minimum activity thresholds and toxicity limits.

4

Execute Analysis

Submit the task and wait for the platform's AI model to process. Complex analyses may take several minutes to complete.

5

View & Download Results

Once the analysis is complete, view the detailed report on the results page and download relevant data in multiple formats (CSV, FASTA, PDF).

Note: This is a general operational flow. Specific steps may vary by function. Please refer to the help documentation for each functional module for details.

Our models are trained on extensive experimental data and exhibit high prediction accuracy. For activity prediction, our current models achieve approximately 85-90% accuracy on validation datasets.
